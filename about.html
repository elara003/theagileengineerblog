About Eddy Lara

Software engineer, architect, and leader passionate about building systems that work

Who I Am

I'm a software engineer and architect with over a decade of experience building and scaling distributed systems. I've worked across different industries—from financial services to e-commerce to healthcare—and I've learned that the fundamentals of good software engineering remain constant even as technologies change.

My focus is on the intersection of technology and team dynamics. The best technical solutions mean nothing if your team can't implement and maintain them. Similarly, the most efficient team processes fall apart if the underlying technology doesn't support them.

What Drives Me

I'm passionate about turning complex business problems into simple, maintainable solutions. Too often, I see teams adding complexity when what they really need is clarity. My goal is always to find the simplest thing that could possibly work, and then evolve from there.

I believe in:

  • Pragmatic architecture - patterns that solve real problems, not just look good on whiteboards
  • Sustainable development - code and practices that teams can maintain over years, not just ship
  • Continuous learning - staying curious about new technologies while remaining grounded in fundamentals
  • Knowledge sharing - making complex concepts accessible to teams at all levels

My Approach

When I encounter a new system or problem, I start with questions, not solutions:

  • What are we actually trying to achieve?
  • What are the real constraints we're working within?
  • How will this system evolve over time?
  • Who will maintain this, and what's their context?

Only after understanding the problem space do I start thinking about technical solutions. And even then, I prefer boring, well-understood technologies over shiny new ones—unless there's a compelling reason to do otherwise.
